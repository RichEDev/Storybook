Supplier Contacts
Overview

When storing detailed information about suppliers whom you hold contracts with, it is inevitable that during your working relationship, a multitude of individuals with be involved.

Pro-active management of these suppliers hinges on successful communication and ensuring that all involved in the business process and kept informed of who's who and what role they play.

To this end, Framework provides the capability for storing electronic business cards (or Supplier Contacts) for all personnel relating to the management of these suppliers.

The modifying and deleting of individual contacts can be executed by the hyperlinks adjacent to each contact's details. Before deletion is permitted, the user is prompted to confirm the action they wish to take. Click 'OK' to proceed with the deletion or 'Cancel' to abort the action.

To add a new contact, click the 'New' link. The user is transferred to the contact detail page where the related information can be specified.

Note: These contacts relate to the supplier's employees and representatives and not those considered internal contacts.

 Mandatory fields

Field Name Description
Contact Name This is the name of the individual whose details are being stored.

This field must be unique. Should two individuals have the same name, additional information will have to be provided to distinguish one from the other.
Is Main Contact? It is usual that one particular contact amongst all is the key point of contact.

This may be an account manager, engineer etc. This indication allows any other users reviewing the supplier to understand who should be contacted in the first instance, should they be unfamiliar with the organisation.

 Additional Field Functionality

Field Name Description
Email Address Once an email address has been specified, the label to the left of the field will become a hyperlink that will allow the launching of an 'new email' window from the users default email package to the indicated recipient.
Web Address Once a web address has been specified and stored in the database, the label to the left of the field becomes a hyperlink. Clicking the hyperlink will open a browser window and open the specified web page (assuming an internet connection is present).
