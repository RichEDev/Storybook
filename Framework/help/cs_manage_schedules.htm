Schedule Management
Overview

Should a contract be modified, re-negotiated or have any of it's terms and conditions changed for whatever reason, then this may result in a new 'schedule' of the contract. The new schedule will supercede the one it is replacing and will therefore have a different value in the provided 'Schedule Number' field.

Note: Depending on the particular action you have actioned, please expand and refer to the sections below that will outline each scenario in more detail.

Regardless of whether you have actioned a 'New Schedule', 'Updated the current Schedule' or 'Merged a Schedule', the resultant new contract schedule record will have it's 'Schedule Number' field suffixed with '/new'.

Once a '/new' schedule has been created by the system, the original(s) will have to be archived manually by the user by changing their 'Contract Status', otherwise they will remain an active (live) contract.

 Add New Schedule

When should I use this option?

Scenario: A contract has had Terms & Conditions changed or had a new product added to the contract. This will typically incur a new schedule.

How do I create a new schedule?

  • Access the contract that you wish to create the new schedule for.

  • Click the 'Add New Schedule' option under the Contract Menu

  • A duplicate of the schedule will be create with a Schedule Number suffix of /new

    • What information is duplicated?

    This option duplicates all the core contract details, contract products, attachments and staff notifies.

    Tip: If a complete copy of the contract schedule is required (in effect a 'Save As New' action), then the 'Update Schedule' option should be used.
     Create Schedule Links

    When should I use this option?

    Scenario: You have three contracts already defined within the database that are schedules of the same contract. They are not currently 'linked' together as part of a schedule chain.

    How do I create the schedule links?

  • Type a part or all of the 'Contract Description' field to search for.

  • A second box allows an optional alternative search string should you be attempting to link to contracts with very different contract descriptions.

  • Click the 'Search' button

  • A list of contracts matching the description will be displayed.

  • Select the contracts to be linked together as part of the schedule chain by ticking the checkbox to the left of the contract.

  • Click the 'Create Links' button below the list of contracts.

    • A status box will detail the number of contracts added to the new schedule chain.

    Note: A minimum of two contracts must be ticked for a successful schedule chain to be created.
     Merge Schedules

    When should I use this option?

    Scenario: You have two or more contract schedules with a vendor, each with different products or services. This will typically happen because the products were purchased at different times.

    To bring the renewal dates or invoicing to a single transaction, a new schedule is required that pools the different schedules together under one contract schedule.

    What information is carried across during the merge?

    As part of the merge process, contract detail is taken from the nominated 'Primary' contract.

    All contract notes, contract products, electronic attachments are duplicated and merged under the newly created contract.

    How do I merge my contracts?

  • Access any contract that belongs to the schedule chain.

  • Select 'Merge Schedules' from the menu

  • All contracts in the schedule chain are then displayed. The row colour indicates whether they are archived or live contracts.

  • One schedule has to be nominated as the 'Primary' schedule. This schedule will have its core contract detail used for the new schedule being created.

  • All other contracts that are to be merged, should be selected by ticking the box in the second column.

  • Click 'Perform Merge' to action the merge process.

    • Note: The 'Primary' schedule must be one of the contracts selected for merging otherwise the merge process will not proceed.
     Update Schedule

    When should I use this option?

    Scenario: You have three contracts already defined within the database that are schedules of the same contract. They are not currently 'linked' together as part of a schedule chain.

    What does 'Update Schedule' do?

    This option will perform a complete duplication of the selected schedule, leaving the duplicate copy with a /new suffix after the schedule number.

    All contract details, associated products, notes, invoices, attachments and forecasts are carried across to the new schedule.

    How does this differ from 'Add New Schedule'?

    'Add New Schedule' doesn't carry across all associated information. Only the contract details, contract products and attachments will be carried across. All financial data remains associated with the original.

    Tip: Update Schedule could also be thought of as a 'Save As New' function
     Link Existing Schedules

    When should I use this option?

    Scenario: If you have a series of contract schedules already linked in a chain, but wish other contracts in the database to be included into the schedule chain.

    This option allows the selection and inclusion into the schedule chain of currently unlinked contracts.

    How do I add the new schedules to the chain?

  • Type a part or all of the 'Contract Description' field to search for.

  • A second box allows an optional alternative search string should you be attempting to link to contracts with very different contract descriptions.

  • Click the 'Search' button

  • A list of contracts matching the description will be displayed (exluding any already linked in a chain).

  • Select the contracts to be linked together as part of the schedule chain by ticking the checkbox to the left of the contract.

  • Click the 'Add Links' button below the list of contracts.

    • A status box will detail the number of contracts added to the new schedule chain.

     Remove Current Schedule Link

    What does this option do?

    If a contract schedule is part of a schedule chain, but no longer requires to be so, this option allows you to exclude the currently selected contract from the schedule chain.

    How do I remove the Schedule Link?

  • Location the contract schedule to be removed by using the 'Next' and 'Previous' buttons to navigate.

  • Click the 'Remove Current Schedule Link' menu option.

  • Once the screen has refreshed, you will see that the 'Next' and 'Previous' are no longer present.

  • The schedule link has been successfully removed and the contract is stand-alone once more.
